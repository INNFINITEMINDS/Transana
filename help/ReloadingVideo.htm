Reloading your Video

Now that you've gotten your transcript finished and time-coded, you are probably ready for a short break. Quit Transana. If you did not save your transcript first, notice that Transana prompts you to save it before the program stops running. When you are ready to return to this tutorial, follow these directions to reload your video.

To reload your video and continue working, the first thing you need to do is locate the transcript you want to work on. Although Transana is designed for analyzing video, the basic unit of analysis is actually the Transcript. This is because a single Episode (video) might have more than one Transcript in order to be analyzed in more than one way. For example, one transcript may represent field notes, a second transcript could be a verbatim record of the portions that the principle investigator thinks are interesting, and a third, phoneme-based transcript may cover a different part of the video that a graduate student is analyzing. In this circumstance, you have one Episode, but Transana must know which of the three transcripts you want to work with. Thus, you load a video by selecting one of the associated transcripts.

Activity: To reload the sample video, use the Data window. Follow these directions:

1. Click the + next to the Series node. (Mac users will see a triangle instead of a "+".) You see all the series you've created.

2. Click the + next to the series called Sample. You see all the episodes in the Sample series.

3. Click the + next to the episode called Sample Video. You see all the transcripts for the episiode Sample.

4. Double-click Sample Transcript.

(NOTE: Transana has the capacity to display more than one transcript at a time. See Working with Multiple Transcripts in the manual for more information.)
