Transana's Organizational Structure

Transana is designed to allow researchers to work with large collections of media files. It provides various tools and metaphors for organizing, analyzing, and retrieving media files, segments of media files, and transcriptions of media files. It also allows you to link a transcription to a media file so that you can move seamlessly through both at the same time.

Each of your original media files is represented in Transana's database as an episode. An episode entry in the Transana database contains information about a single digital media file. You group episodes into series; series describe the logical connection among sets of episodes. The Series node in Transana is where you organize your raw media data.

The basic unit of analysis in Transana is the clip, which is a portion of an episode that you identify as analytically meaningful. Clips are organized into thematic collections. With time, your collection structure will likely reflect the theoretical understanding of your data you develop through your qualitative analysis. Analytic data, extracted from raw data, falls under the Collections node within Transana.

Clips are flagged for complex analysis through the assignment of keywords. A keyword is a term that you define that represents a concept that you want to represent within your data or study. Clips can have multiple keywords. Keywords are organized into keyword groups. Your keyword structure also reflects the theoretical structure of your qualitative analysis. Your coding structure is developed under the Keywords node within Transana.

For more information about how data is organized in Transana, please see Organizational Structure in the Transana Manual.
