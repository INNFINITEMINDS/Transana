Transana's Organizational Structure

Transana is designed to allow researchers to work with large collections of data files. It provides various tools and metaphors for organizing, analyzing, and retrieving data, analytically important segments of these files, and transcripts associated with media files.

The Library node in Transana is where you organize your source or raw text and media data in Transana. Each of your original text files is imported as a Document in Transana, and each of your source media files is represented in Transana as an Episode. An Episode contains a media file, (or can contain more than one overlapping media file, as from multiple cameras recording the same events,) and can be associated with one or more Transcripts that are linked to the media file contents. You group related Documents and Episodes into Libraries; the Library describes the logical connection among sets of Documents and Episodes.

Analytic data, extracted from raw data, falls under the Collections node within Transana. The basic units of analysis in Transana are the Quote for text, the Clip for media data, and the Snapshot for still image data. Quotes and Clips are portions of Documents and Episodes that you identify as analytically meaningful. Snapshots represent still image data you bring into your analysis. Quotes, Clips, and Snapshots are organized into thematic Collections. With time, your Collection structure will likely reflect the theoretical understanding of your data you develop through your qualitative analysis. The Collection containging a Quote, Clip, or Snapshot indicates one aspect of what is analytically significance about that Quote, Clip, or Snapshot.

Your coding structure is developed under the Keywords node within Transana. Quotes, Clips, and Snapshots can be coded through the assignment of Keywords. A Keyword is a label that you define that represents a concept that you want to represent within your data or study. Quotes, Clips, and Snapshots can have multiple Keywords. Keywords are organized into Keyword Groups. Your Keyword structure can also reflect the theoretical structure of your qualitative analysis.
