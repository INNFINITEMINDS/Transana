Making a Presentation

Once you have used Transana to transcribe and analyze your data, the final step is to share what you have found with others. To facilitate this, Transana has a Presentation Mode, designed to help you share your analytic data with others.

The primary use of Presentation Mode is to share video data with others. In Presentation Mode, Transana can use the full screen to play a video, can display only the Media Window (across the top of the screen) and the Document Window (across the bottom), or can play your video's audio while displaying a transcript but hiding the video. This last mode is useful when subject confidentiality is an issue and you feel it would be better to avoid showing your subject's face.

Activity: To use Presentation Mode, follow these directions.

1. On the Options menu, click Presentation Mode. Then click Media Only.

2. Double-click the name of a Clip (Create Clip by double-clicking Collection, for example) and click the play button on the Media Window control bar. Transana uses the entire monitor to play the Clip. When the Clip finishes playing, the other windows return.

3. Try selecting Media and Transcript Only instead of Media Only. Notice the Document Window at the bottom of the screen. If the Transcript text is not large enough for your intended audience, you may wish to enlarge the text in your Clip Trancript(s) prior to doing your presentation.

4. Now try Audio and Transcript Only with a video Clip.

To return to regular operation, on the Options menu, click Presentation Mode, then click All Windows.
